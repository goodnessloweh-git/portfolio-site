My Story

Learning Journey

Why I started, what I've built, and the kind of engineer I'm becoming.

Why I Joined the Program

I come from a background in logistics and supply chain management. I spent years watching operations break down because of manual processes, poor handoffs, and systems that weren't talking to each other. Technology was always the thread I followed — and DevOps gave me the language to follow it all the way through. I joined this program because I wanted to be on the engineering side of those solutions. Not just a user of tools, but someone who builds and maintains the infrastructure that keeps things running.

What I've Learned So Far

I've gone from SSH-ing into my first EC2 instance and not knowing what I was looking at, to provisioning EKS clusters with Terraform, writing multi-node Ansible playbooks, and building CI/CD pipelines that run without anyone touching a server. I've broken things in production, responded to a real security incident, debugged multi-region networking failures, and kept going. Every error message I've ever fixed taught me more than the tutorials that never showed me what failure looks like. I'm comfortable being uncomfortable — and that's not something a course taught me. The projects did.

The Engineer I'm Becoming

I want to become a DevOps engineer who builds infrastructure that other developers love working with — secure, observable, automated from day one, and well-documented. I also want to be a bridge: bringing more African women into cloud and DevOps through She Builds Tech. Engineering isn't just a career path for me. It's something I'm building a life around — and I'm just getting started.
